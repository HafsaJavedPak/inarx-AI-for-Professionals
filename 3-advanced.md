
# What Enterprise AI Is and How It Differs from Team-Level AI
## Part 1- Not Just Bigger, but Different
By now you have built AI tools that work for you and for your team — a custom assistant that handles a specialised task, an automation that connects a few apps, perhaps a shared workflow the whole team relies on. At team level, a handful of people agree on how a tool should work, and if something breaks they fix it between themselves.
Enterprise AI is a different thing entirely. It is not simply team AI made bigger. When AI operates across a whole organisation — thousands of people, many departments, systems the business cannot run without — the change is not one of size but of kind.
Think of the difference between a single delivery van and a national logistics network. The network is not just more vans. It needs schedules, depots, rules, and people whose whole job is to keep it running safely. Enterprise AI works the same way.

```text
+ADD
Consider a global bank. A single branch team might build a chatbot to help its own staff answer common customer questions faster — a handful of people agree on how it works, and if it misbehaves, they simply turn it off or fix it. Now scale that to the whole bank: thousands of employees across hundreds of branches and call centers, in different countries, under different regulations, all relying on AI to handle customer queries, flag fraud, and support loan decisions. A single wrong or biased answer is no longer a minor glitch fixed between colleagues — it can mean regulatory fines, reputational damage, or unequal treatment of customers across an entire country. That is the leap from team AI to enterprise AI.
```

### Key Idea: Enterprise AI is not team AI at a larger scale. The jump in size brings a jump in complexity, integration, governance, and risk.
